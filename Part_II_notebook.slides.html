Ford GoBike System Data Exploration

by Hamza Mohamed

Investigation Overview

Goal: Uncover patterns in bike-sharing usage to differentiate behavior between subscribers and casual users.
Key Insights:

  1. Subscribers take shorter, weekday-focused trips (commuters).
  2. Casual users take longer trips, peaking on weekends (leisure).
  3. Age has minimal impact on trip duration but correlates with user type.

Dataset Overview

Source: Ford GoBike System (February 2019)
Records: 183,412 trips after cleaning.
Key Features:

  • duration_min: Trip duration in minutes (log-transformed for analysis).
  • user_type: Subscriber (85% of users) vs. Customer (15%).
  • start_day, start_hour: Peak usage on weekdays (Thursdays) at 8 AM and 5 PM.

Executive Summary:

  • Most trips are short (median: 8 mins for subscribers, 14 mins for casual users).
  • Peak usage aligns with commuting hours for subscribers.
  • Age weakly correlates with duration, but user type is the dominant factor.

Trip Duration by User Type

Key Insight:

  • Subscribers (blue) take 70% shorter trips than casual users (orange).
  • Tight interquartile range for subscribers suggests consistent commuting behavior.

Trips by Hour and Day

Key Insight:

  • Subscribers (commuters) dominate weekday rush hours (8 AM and 5 PM).
  • Casual users show increased activity on weekends at midday (12 PM).
  • Operational teams should prioritize bike redistribution during these peak times.

Age vs. Duration by User Type

Key Takeaways:

  • Consistency: Subscribers take short, predictable trips daily (89 mins), ideal for commutes.
  • Weekend Leisure: Casual users trips double in duration on weekends (1416 mins), highlighting leisure use.
  • Actionable Insight: Boost bike availability on weekends for casual users and optimize weekday maintenance for subscribers.
